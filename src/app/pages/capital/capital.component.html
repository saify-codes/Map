Albany, New York

The Capital City of the Empire State

City Information

Albany, the capital of New York State, is a city rich in history, culture, and government influence. It serves as the political hub of New York and has grown into a city full of vibrant communities and diverse industries.

Key Facts about Albany

Population

As of the 2020 census, Albany has a population of approximately 97,000 people.

Year Incorporated

Albany was officially incorporated as a city in 1686, making it one of the oldest cities in the United States.

Region

Albany is located in the Capital District of New York, in the eastern part of the state.

Classification

Albany is considered an urban city with a mix of government and residential areas.

Average Income

The average income in Albany is lower than the statewide average. The median household income is about $60,000, compared to the state average of around $70,000.
